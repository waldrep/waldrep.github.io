About the Project

As part of the first generation of the Fulbright - National Geographic Digital Storytelling Fellowship, author Michael Waldrep spent 9 months in Mexico City, North America's largest metropolitan area, investigating the roots and contemporary status of the urban form of that city's sprawl. The project, La Ciudad Actual (or The Contemporary City) published here, is an assemblage of photography, video, mapping and writing that seeks to catalog a city in flux, and to describe some of the processes here that are seen throughout rapidly urbanising cities throughout the world.

About the Author

Michael Waldrep is a documentary artist and researcher focused on cities. A Los Angeles native, he holds degrees in Film Studies and City Planning from UC Berkeley and MIT.

Michael Waldrep is an artist and researcher based in Berlin and Los Angeles.
Through experimental cartography, filmmaking, and photography, his practice is focused on architecture, urban design, and the ways in which societies create and occupy space.

Michael was educated in Film and City Planning at UC Berkeley and MIT, and has been a Fulbright National Geographic grantee in Mexico City, a Harvard GSD - Richards Rogers fellow in London and from 2017-2022, a core member of Studio Olafur Eliasson in Berlin.

Michael works in English, Spanish, and German, and is currently teaching narrative filmmaking in the architecture department at the Universität Kassel. In 2024, he’s pursuing freelance work and elaborating a project on the built traces of the offshore economy in Luxembourg, South Dakota, and Delaware.
